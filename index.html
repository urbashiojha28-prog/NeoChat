📚 Chapter 9: Cell Biology & Mitosis

Biology Notes · Unit 3 · March 2026 CBSEClass 12
📌 Key Concept: Mitosis results in two daughter cells with the same number of chromosomes as the parent cell.

9.1 Introduction

Cell division is the process by which a parent cell divides into two or more daughter cells. In eukaryotes, it occurs as mitosis and meiosis. The cell cycle consists of interphase (G1, S, G2) and the mitotic phase.

9.2 Phases of Mitosis

  • Prophase: Chromatin condenses, nuclear envelope breaks down.
  • Metaphase: Chromosomes align at the metaphase plate.
  • Anaphase: Sister chromatids pulled to opposite poles.
  • Telophase: Nuclear envelopes re-form.
  • Cytokinesis: Two genetically identical daughter cells form.
