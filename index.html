📚 Chapter 9: Cell Biology & Mitosis

Biology Notes · Unit 3 · March 2026

9.1 Introduction

Cell division is the process by which a parent cell divides into two or more daughter cells. In eukaryotes it occurs as mitosis and meiosis.

9.2 Phases of Mitosis

  • Prophase: Chromatin condenses, nuclear envelope breaks down.
  • Metaphase: Chromosomes align at the metaphase plate.
  • Anaphase: Sister chromatids pulled to opposite poles.
  • Telophase: Nuclear envelopes re-form.
  • Cytokinesis: Two daughter cells form.
